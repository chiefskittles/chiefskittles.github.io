DJ Myosuke

With appreciation and thanks to Okami & Rems - Released Apr. 2020

Another stream-focused pack, and another pack dedicated to a single artist (if the title didn't tip you off). This pack was initially started to be part of a series of packs headed by Okami named "Stamina Factory," with each entry in the series being dedicated to a different artist, crafted by a different step artist. Unfortunately, the series was ultimately unable to take flight, but by that time, the majority of this pack had already been written, so I finished it up regardless.

And then I restepped it, because most of it was... really bad.

And then I restepped it again, because it was still bad.

So, in the end, this one took a lot of effort to get just right, and in spending said effort, I ended up learning a ton about how to pattern streams well at all speeds (coupled with the one-offs and singles I was doing on the side for the fof crowd) and formed a rough, but lovingly-crafted foundation for me to become adept at charting not just tech, but stream files as well.

The ultimate capstone to this pack is "Promised Everland," an original, 20-minute long mixtape created by the master himself, Rems. This marathon serves as the "boss" of the pack and is, naturally, significantly more challenging to play and pass than the other charts present. I strongly encourage you to listen to the mix even if you're not interested in playing the chart - it is absolutely phenomenal.

Tags: Stream, Single Artist, Marathon

Skittles Selection 7

"Possibly the best tech pack of 2019" - Released Apr. 2019

Yes, someone said that about this pack. No, I didn't pay them to say it. This pack contains some of my best work thus far and marks the point where I feel like I really locked in my own personal style of technical writing.


Highlights and notable tournament files include Arcology on Permafrost, Bubble in the Air, Estrangement, Giga Thrash, Glitched Character, Panjandrum, Quality Control, Samplinglitch, and Zephyr.



Yes, that is almost half the pack. I wasn't kidding when I said this is some of my best work.



Tags: Technical, HS, Full Difficulties

Skittles Selection 6

We ECFA now - Released Jan. 2019

Finally, competence emerges. This pack is when I began specifically leaning towards "tournament-style" writing in my simfiles. Consequently, this is when some of my files began to make infrequent appearances in tournament packs, such as Rumble in the Prairie, DDR Storm, and East Coast FA. This pack was also when I first attempted to explore the idea of a supplementary scale to better quantify a chart's "scoring" difficulty separate from its passing difficulty (something that several people began to develop around this time) and the """humble""" beginnings of my tendency to write comically-long readmes for all of my packs.

As you might imagine from the description, the majority of the files in this pack range from moderately to exceptionally technical, with few stream or "basic" files present.

Highlights include Dual Emotion, Flashdance, Lonely Witch, Travel to Future, and Zombie Stomp.


Tags: Technical, Full Difficulties

Skittles' Ancient Garbage

We all start somewhere. I started in the dumpster.

These are all my old files. The vast majority of them are utter trash. I advise you to avoid them entirely.


Prior to now, I was adamant that these no longer be distributed for a large number of reasons, the most obvious of which is that they all suck. Enormously. However, enough people have asked me about seeing these that I have decided to acquiese and allow them to be downloaded once again.


IMPORTANT NOTE - I CANNOT STRESS THIS ENOUGH


These files were all made far, far before I really got a good grip on proper charting. As such, I cannot guarantee that any of these files are synced well, flow properly, or are even fun. Again, I REALLY encourage you to play my newer files instead. I do not have the time nor the desire to return to any of these and improve any facet of them at all.

Tags: These, Files, Are, All, Bad
